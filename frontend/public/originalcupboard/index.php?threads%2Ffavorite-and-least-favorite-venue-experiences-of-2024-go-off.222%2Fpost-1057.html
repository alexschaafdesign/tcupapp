I think my fav show is gonna be my most recent bats show at icehouse. but not much to do with the venue, cuz they were being kinda weird about the guest list and that stressed me out. moreso becuz my band totally locked in which felt so sweet and the other bands were so kind, fun, and inspiring.

my least fav mightve been a show at aster becuz we played last and they have a hard cut off at 10pm and we had to ask the second band to wrap up their set early so we could have time to play and then the sound tech was grumpy with US about making sure to end on time before we even started playing. it just sucked to feel like we had to advocate to even play a short set at all, then the sound tech was sassy with us like we had anything to do with the timing of the night. booo. amazing what an advance with our set length and times couldve done for us in that moment

Terminal used to be a good, solid venue ten years ago. It was still a shithole and you could smell the men's room from onstage, but it was easy to pack and a chill environment. Sound was adequate, if not decent. Sad to hear it's gone downhill.

Only played a couple gigs this year, but as a weirdo two-piece I dig White Squirrel. Sound guy was great. Plenty of stage space for us (and someone else's equipment ) and a good bar.

My worst venue experiences don't exist any more. So many have shut down in the last 2 decades.

Rachael G said:
I'll go first:

Hook and Ladder, specifically because of Corinne Couette. Not only because they operate as a non profit, they have their cards in order as far as advances and finance breakdowns, and the staff there is amazing of course. Mission Room should open the garage door and do live shows this summer IMO.

It has been really hard to have a *what-should-be-a-poppin show* at Uptown VFW. They barely promote and leave it largely up to the artist to distribute promotion, even though they have a robust local patron crowd that could potentially benefit from comp'd or lowered show tickets. Willing to work with them to achieve a more equitable payout and a potentially larger turn out, which benefits us all.
