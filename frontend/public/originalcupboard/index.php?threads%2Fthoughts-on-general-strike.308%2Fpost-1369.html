Would love this not to be the case but the working class is nowhere near as organized as would be necessary to pull off a successful General Strike as popularly conceived. Previously in history, the concept of a General Strike grew out of a labor movement that was more coordinated, more organized, more physically concentrated, less alienated, and had a mass participation in the form of unions, social organizations, co-ops etc. It wasn't from eleven million people spontaneously deciding to strike. Labor needs to be organized to show up in the numbers and with the discipline necessary to make a General Strike effective. Not saying not to sign this card, but without any vanguard organization to coordinate, the millions of people necessary to make it effective aren't going to mobilize. It's putting the cart before the horse.

HOWEVER this is where TCUP comes in. Down the line, this is a space where we as performers can stand in solidarity alongside other organizations and unions of proles to be part of an effective, coordinated bloc of actors. I'd say focusing our efforts on TCUP and within other organizations are the way to materialize this as the rubber meets the road. And all of this said, the less money in the pockets of these freaks the better! Stochastic boycotts, wildcat strikes, work slowdowns/stoppages, just buying less shit, anything that throws some wrench in the gears of capital, no matter how small, is a net good.

I'm not anywhere near an expert on unionizing, strikes, or the labor movement, but my instincts tell me that for a strike of that magnitude to be effective, you'd hear about it in person. Also, I'm not saying this website is illegitimate (they have some strong sponsor organizations from what I can see) but I get the feeling there MIGHT BE fake "fight the power" websites or even organizations trying to capitalize on the current confusion and take advantage of people in a general sense. I'm not an expert on any of this and this is all based on instinct, but I did feel compelled to share my two cents about it-- all I can say is vet your sources and trust your gut

It's a tricky thing, because I want to support action being taken beyond the "usual suspects" of the big national orgs who usually call mass actions and events; the fact that the co-sponsors of this are a bunch of smaller groups I'm not familiar with, a podcast, and a bunch of influencers isn't necessarily a bad or disqualifying thing. At the same time, these kinds of calls for a general strike pop up kind of all the time, and there usually isn't much actual *organizing* work behind them—more often just lots of social media posts. Maybe this one is different! I honestly don't know, and it seems like they have more of a plan/process than others I've seen. I also think part of navigating this historical moment is "try stuff," that experimentation and new approaches are needed.

All that being said, a compare/contrast might be with the UAW's ongoing plan for a 2028 general strike. On one hand, that's a long time from now, and a lot of harm is going to be done between now and then. On the other hand, having years of lead time to organize and coordinate, plus being deeply connected to the labor movement—those two variables really matter, I think.
