Thoughts and ravings: finding success in a post-social-media world

  • Start date Jan 21, 2025

With Meta, Twitter, (and now TikTok) being controlled by evil oligarchs who want to take our rights away, I've been ruminating a lot on how we as musicians and creatives are going to continue to find reach and success. Will we return to boots-on-the-ground marketing and physical media? Will we continue to use these billionaire-run apps since they are the best way to get people to see our music? Obviously I am thinking in extremes, but I think it is worth discussing!

Are there ways you all have successfully marketed and/or sold your music on platforms other than TikTok, Instagram, Twitter, etc? Do you have any wisdom or ideas on how we can move forward as creatives? Or maybe you, like me, just need to vent about this.

(P.S. I am excited for the future of this platform! I acknowledge that this is an awesome place to promote music, but I'm definitely thinking bigger picture too!)

Mike (the audio engineer for this project) texted me this morning raging against Meta, and we were discussing what a potential move to a different main social platform would look like and how to reconnect with the MN scene once there.

We have been following along with the TCUP journey throughout the year. So today, after our conversation, I started to connect the dots, and I thought, "TCUP would probably be a great home for a local music forum, I bet other people are sick of Meta and X..."

So when I got home from work and discovered that this forum already exists, I was pretty excited to start a profile.

I plan to be active in checking in here, and to recommend this forum as an alternative to all my friends and collaborators!

However, I don't have any answers to your question. We use YouTube, which we're finding it slower-going in building an audience (and I'm not sure if Alphabet is much better that Meta). We've been experimenting with stickers and printed 8.5x11 posters around town, but you don't get metrics from posterboards, so who knows if that drives audiences...

We decided to include our IG network in the conversation, and I made a poll asking about social media preference in a story. So far, most respondents voted that Instagram remains the best place to connect with the MN music scene. If I remember, I'll post the final results when the poll is over. Instagram users may be biased toward Instagram! But, in my experience, IG has been the "home" of the local scene for years.

Does anyone use Bluesky? That has been suggested as an alternative

I’ve been thinking about this a lot too! Sleeping Jesus has an email list, it’s a lot less folks (maybe have 50-75 signed up right now) but it’s a lot more direct and probably about that many people see our stuff thanks to the algorithm anyways lol

I’m only really on insta to check out local releases and keep in the loop - I check out Ear Coffee, Melodic Noise, the current, and Radio K for that, but a lot fall through the cracks. So as more folks are on here and sharing, hopefully needing socials for that will be less needed.

Most of Sleepy’s steps forward haven’t come from social media, it’s from playing a lot of shows, making in-person connections out and about, and non-social media distribution (Tiger Bomb radio promo and Groover (I think?) streaming distribution have been great). So I have hope!

Honestly, I’ve been trying to get as many people’s phone numbers when we meet or reconnect, and simply texting them when I have a show or new releases to share has also been a future tactic I’ve been thinking of.

A free Patreon for sharing dates and releases, or a paid one if you have a lot of extra goodies to offer, is also on my mind to look into! Substack seems interesting too, but not sure its effectiveness for just sharing info. An ongoing “artist journal” could be a cool vibe.

Great topic and i've been thinking about it lately a lot too. I think things have been trending this way for a while (frustration with how we're supposed to market ourselves on social media) and maybe this oligarchy takeover will be a much-needed push to finally make some moves.

For me, i've generally shifted to become way more locally-focused, rather than paying for FB ads in California/Oregon or something, which makes other forms of promotion way more attractive / relevant.

i can envision a mix of email lists (very much a fan of), the Cupboard (host your own band profile (with a list of your shows included, have people subscribe to your feed there, message you through that, etc)), and also more intensive in-person/physical promo.

for the third point, the 'Flyering Location' list i have started here was sort of with that in mind, it'd be sweet to have some sort of rotating 'street team' where we all help each other put up flyers and promote things in person/physically?? something like that??

When i restarted my email newsletter (after getting my FB/IG disabled after i got hacked) , i got a ton of feedback from people that were very psyched about it, "I'm not even on instagram anymore so i had no idea what you were up to", etc. Substack has worked well for me, mostly because it was the only one that could import a big list of emails for free (i don't charge anything to read it either) -- and a 21% open rate seems way way way higher than the number of people that see an instagram post/story? (the email list is so big because it's like 15 years worth of getting emails from bandcamp downloads/purchases lol)

idk, it could get shitty but for now it's been all upside for me at least. and maybe i'm more suited for this because my 'crowd' is slightly older, i don't know if younger folks would be as pumped about getting an email lol.

I've been growing my newsletter via Substack, both paid and free, for a year now and it's definitely my favorite way to connect with people. My newsletter signees are like ACTUALLY very invested in my silly little bullshit, regardless of their subscription status, and much more likely to act on show announcements, merch offerings, and writing workshop signups than anybody else on other apps. I don't know if Substack will always be the answer in terms of platforms - it has its own nasty little moments - but I *do* think that newsletters are the vibe!!

I'll also say that if you're a queer performer, you gotta be posting about shows on Lex. It's technically a dating app that frankly varies for me between being gd annoying and boring, BUT people on there are looking for stuff to do! Whenever I post about shows there, at least 1 person shows up because of my post.

Just started on Bluesky - if any of yall are on there, let's find each other! I think as an app it has the best shot at being actually interesting and useful (you can post links and talk about stuff without getting buried, for instance, bc it's open source), at least as it stands now.
