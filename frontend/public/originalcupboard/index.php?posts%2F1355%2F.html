Caydence expecting "Pay to Play" for a Benefit Show for Aid--Negative Experience with booking

  • Start date Feb 4, 2025

Hey all!

I was very disappointed that a booker with Caydence expected us to "pay to play" for a show that is intended to raise aid for community members. The message I received felt extremely disrespectful and made me feel humiliated when all of us are trying to organize and do a good thing.

Back story, we recently got in touch with Caydence to host a benefit show with a bill full of young folks ages 17-24. The show was initially going to be for LA fire relief aid as we started planning it a few weeks back (but we have now shifted to raise funds for unhoused community members, though this wasn't yet communicated to the booker). We made the cause and our intentions clear. For some more background, my band Oceanographer previously played at Caydence with Sine Iris and literally packed the place. The barista and sound staff told us to "come back anytime" because we had such a good draw. We didn't have to pay a retainer when booking that show.

When booking this show, however, I was told that Caydence would need $200 "rental" for paying staff and covering their costs for the night. This seemed completely understandable, and I misunderstood the email, thinking they would take that out of our door proceeds that night, with the hope that all the remaining profit would go to Ventura County Wildlife Relief. Then I receieved this email:



I was pretty confused, and a person who I got this connection through who was assisting on organizing the show suggested I request if he'd "float" the $200 for the cause, and have them take it out of our door cut as we initially thought would be the case. I explained that everyone organizing this show is a group of young broke musicians, and that I'm not some promoter organizing this. I mentioned that we don't have the financial means to pay that $200 upfront, and also as a musician who has gigged all over town, that this was a new experience for me. I have been struggling with finances lately--as everyone has in this crazy inflation--and was embarrassed to share this, and then I received this email that made me feel significantly worse about the matter:



What happened to "come back anytime", when we initially packed the place and made Caydence a decent amount of money on a weekday night? Also, in what world is Caydence actually a 99 capacity--try 50.

This was so disheartening to experience as someone trying to use my passions for good and just made me feel like an idiot for not having $200 upfront. I wanted everyone at TCUP to know about this so no one else experiences it.

I've rented theater spaces to put on shows many times and it's a great way to produce a show in a more grandiose way or a way that elevates your music and gives you a greater degree of control over your environment.
I would not consider doing that at any "regular" bar or cafe venue. If it's a rental, there are way cooler places to risk your own money.
Having a minimum taken out of the door is understandable, as are costs of running a business, but one rents a theater or hall. One does not rent a bar. You book it and it either works out or it doesn't. Afterwards the relationship either continues for more events or it doesn't.

I've played Cadence once and never again. They didn't have stage monitors that night so the onstage sound was bad. The sound person was one of the owners and there was one staff person behind the counter and the door person. We had 2 solo acoustic acts, my band and the headliner on the bill that didn't even go through the sound system. They played acoustic guitars on the floor in front of the stage and sang without mics. We only had a vocal mic. We went on third and after us the sound person was essentially done. There was a minor snow storm that night that limited the attendance to about 25 to 30 people. Between the up front fee of $200 plus the additional 30% they also took I figure out they got about 80% of the $10 cover. The owner could have at least waived $100 to $150 of the up front fee since he did minimal sound that night. I'm not using this opportunity to bitch about the venue but rather alert you to how really unethical the venue is when it comes to musician compensation. I would highly suggest finding a different venue. I also advise others to stay away from the venue in general.
