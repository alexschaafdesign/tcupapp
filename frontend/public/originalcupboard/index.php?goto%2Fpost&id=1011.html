I'll go first:

Hook and Ladder, specifically because of Corinne Couette. Not only because they operate as a non profit, they have their cards in order as far as advances and finance breakdowns, and the staff there is amazing of course. Mission Room should open the garage door and do live shows this summer IMO.

It has been really hard to have a *what-should-be-a-poppin show* at Uptown VFW. They barely promote and leave it largely up to the artist to distribute promotion, even though they have a robust local patron crowd that could potentially benefit from comp'd or lowered show tickets. Willing to work with them to achieve a more equitable payout and a potentially larger turn out, which benefits us all.

One of the better experiences I had this year was Berlin in terms of pay, hospitality and tech. They give you a free drink, as much as you can eat of the restaurants “family” meal for the staff and crew, and the greenroom is well stocked with nice wine, whisky, Red Bull, still and bubbly water. It’s also a nice reprieve from the upstairs if you are feeling antisocial/overloaded sensory wise. The sound tech was super nice, accommodating and a step ahead of me most of the time. It sounded phenomenal in there.

Worst experience was probably terminal bar. The gig I played we were the only band as it was a comedy and music thing, and even so, the sound person no showed, they hired a random guy who worked there long ago and just happened to be there. Half their in house cords for mics and PA didn’t work. Sound was ok all said and done, and I generally like the stage and layout, just seems like the venue is a big afterthought.

We just played No Name last weekend and it was my first experience there. Absolutely worth the drive in every way. That place is really nice; they have a hazer and decent lighting, a good size stage and a sense of decor and aesthetics I was not necessarily expecting. They treated us really well and I would play there again any time.
Also had good experiences with Zhora Darling, Turf, Cloudland and White Squirrel.
My main beef with some of the newer venues is just WAY too tiny of stages to be functional. Mission Room for sure has that (what's with that tapering trapezoid shape where it's like a foot and a half deep at one end? Baffling; I'd rather just play on the floor, which we often do in these cases), but it's not the only one. Cloudland, Underground and White Squirrel have that going on too, but other redeeming qualities will keep me interested in playing those spots.
Didn't super love playing at Caydence, but that'll probably be the only time that happens anyway. Nice people at least.
