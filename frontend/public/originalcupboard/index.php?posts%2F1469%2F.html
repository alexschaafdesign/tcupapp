If this is the wrong area to post this information, please make me aware.

Here's the booking info for the Terminal:

Booker's name (last I was aware): Javier
Email: [email protected]
From what I understand, they only have bands Friday and Saturday nights
No clear information on how much of a cut the bar takes, payout in cash
They book a ways out and I have heard they can sometimes double book although this hasn't happened to me. Book with plenty of time and check in before the show to confirm your date. I haven't heard anything bad about the owner, and my interactions with people doing sound has always been fine. Load in through the back door in the back alley. Small-ish room, stage is in the back, sound is good in my experience, I'd take a wild guess and say 30-40 people would fill the place up nicely.

Little Elephant Custom Vinyl:

They allow a no minimum order, so I placed a trial run order for 1 vinyl of our music. I filled out the form for 33 1/3 speed double-sided, provided a track listing, and provided download links to all of our files. They requested the downloads be sent via Dropbox. I did not use the paid version of Dropbox and sent the download links in 4 separate downloads. After I did this, they stated they had received the files. 2 months later, they sent me another email stating they had not received them and requested the files again, so I sent them again via separate downloads again. They stated they had received the files again. When I got this record, it was the correct size (12") but the speed was 45 RPM instead of 33 1/3, which I had requested. 2 songs were also missing on side B. I didn't order any more vinyl from them--there is no way to rate or review them anywhere, either on their site or on Google.

Ethan

I have no experiences about Little Elephant but wanted to add an important detail about the vinyl manufacturing process: lathe-cut vinyl (which Little Elephant does) will always exhibit variable sound quality. That's because every record is cut individually (e.g. a lathe cuts grooves into each record in a run). Pressed records exhibit little sound variability. That's because the "cutting" process is done by etching grooves into a lacquer, which is then sprayed and encased in metal, thus becoming a "stamper." The stamper(s) gets affixed to a press, techs put a glob of vinyl into the press, and voila - out of the press comes a record. Stampers can be used for hundreds (maybe thousands?) of records. The OG copy/paste.

Locally, Outta Wax now does both! The smallest run they do is 20 7" lathe-cut records (5 mins/side), though.
