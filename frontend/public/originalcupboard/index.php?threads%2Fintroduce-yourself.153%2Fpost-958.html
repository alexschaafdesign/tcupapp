KTM/Guante said:
Hey- I'm Kyle Tran Myhre (he/him), the guante from guante & big cats. Signed up here because TCUP's work is very cool, though I've always thought of myself as kind of running parallel to the local music scene rather than really part of it. I'm a full time performer, but it's more on the poetry side - I travel to colleges, conferences, and other community spaces to use poetry/storytelling as a doorway into critical dialogue, often about issues related to consent, counter-narrative masculinity, and violence prevention. My background is in facilitation and critical pedagogy stuff, so it's been a good gig, mixing a creative writing approach with social justice education content. Locally, I was part of the abolitionist group MPD150's core team, and work regularly with arts/culture org TruArtSpeaks.

And then yeah, I've also been an emcee for like 20 years, apart from all that stuff. The new guante & big cats album is called "all dressed up, no funeral," and it's a climate crisis concept album about how we cultivate hope through grief and collective action.

Big thanks to the TCUP team for sharing this space/resource.

Hello everyone! I'm Kevin Priestley and I go by K Priest.

I'm super excited to have learned about this group and effort through Nadi and Katie's interview on Carbon Sound today and immediately wanted to join up and be part of the conversation.

I grew up playing a lot of rock and jazz guitar, but kind of lost creative inspiration until COVID forced me to realize how important playing and making music is to me. These days I produce dance music and dj. I've been steadily releasing my own music via bandcamp and distrokid over the past few years, and I just started a live radio show/vinyl dj residency at Sidecar at the Tap on the first and third Thursdays of the month.

Tonight will be my second gig there and it's a Bring Your Own Vinyl night that we're planning to do each month. Please, please, please come on by any time! I'd love to meet and connect and have this be a place where artists, music-lovers, and industry folks can get together in a low-key environment and share sonic inspiration

Additionally, I've been doing all of this myself (production, mixing, mastering, booking, promo, etc. etc.) while working full time and raising a toddler. It's been a really crazy couple of years, I'm still learning, but I'm happy to help anyone who needs ears on a track, a pat on the back, or a shoulder to lean on.
