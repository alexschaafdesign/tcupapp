Hi everyone! I’m Brian Johnson. I make music under the names BJ Solely and Bill Hando.

Been living in Minneapolis for about 16 years playing in a bunch of different punk bands for most of it. But then COVID hit and I’ve only performed once last December at a songwriters round.

I’m really grateful for finding this group. (Thanks We Are The Willows for posting about it on instagram!) For some reason it’s been really hard to reconnect with my friends in the music scene here. Honestly I think I’m just burned out on all the local shows. Of course being a non drinker 44 year old with autism doesn’t really make hanging out at bars much fun. But I digress…

I’m here because despite my feelings of burnout I still love making music and am always seeking genuine ways to share it and help others make and share too! Lately my focus has shifted to recording. While I’m always busy working on some single, Ep, album, whatever, I would love to get a chance to mix someone else’s songs. That’s one thing I’ve yet to try. If you’re in need of some production assistance let’s talk!

Hey ya’ll! I’m Tyler Steinley, and currently reside in Winona, MN. I’ve been peripherally involved in the Twin Cities scene since 2019 through drumming with my bands Sleeping Jesus and Afflatus. I also produce under the name “Oof”. I’ve already met a lot of you through those avenues, plus working sound at the local Winona spot - No Name Bar.

It’s so inspiring to see community members organize like this, and I’m very excited to be involved from afar. Although the Winona music scene has been reduced a lot post-COVID, we still have a lot of amazing people, places, and events in this small town. Please don’t hesitate to reach out if you’re trying to make a trip down river for a show or collaborations!

My current plan is actually to be moving to ya’ll’s neck of the woods in June - where I’m hoping to get involved much more with TCUP, drum/produce as much as possible, and hopefully find work being a sound engineer as often as I can. I’m already so grateful for this community as it seems like this will be the best starting point for making connections in line with all those goals!

See you out there!

Hey- I'm Kyle Tran Myhre (he/him), the guante from guante & big cats. Signed up here because TCUP's work is very cool, though I've always thought of myself as kind of running parallel to the local music scene rather than really part of it. I'm a full time performer, but it's more on the poetry side - I travel to colleges, conferences, and other community spaces to use poetry/storytelling as a doorway into critical dialogue, often about issues related to consent, counter-narrative masculinity, and violence prevention. My background is in facilitation and critical pedagogy stuff, so it's been a good gig, mixing a creative writing approach with social justice education content. Locally, I was part of the abolitionist group MPD150's core team, and work regularly with arts/culture org TruArtSpeaks.

And then yeah, I've also been an emcee for like 20 years, apart from all that stuff. The new guante & big cats album is called "all dressed up, no funeral," and it's a climate crisis concept album about how we cultivate hope through grief and collective action.

Big thanks to the TCUP team for sharing this space/resource.
